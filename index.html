Research

A section of JWST/NIRCam imagery from detector B4 in the F444W band

Imaging from JWST NIRCam has revealed dust-obscured AGNs with a much higher frequency than previously seen with the rest-frame UV and optical imaging done by the HST. Moreover, the frequency of AGN is higher in the longer wavelength JWST/NIRCam bands such as the F444W. We aim to quantify the frequency of such highly obscured AGN among high redshift galaxies identified as hosts of an AGN using different multi-wavelength tracers. A specific goal of this study is to analyze the morphology of a population of faint radio sources in the COSMOS field in different JWST/NIRCam bands and establish the frequency of highly obscured AGN among them. We plan to do this by fitting all galaxies with a double-Sérsic profile and a Sérsic profile plus a PSF component. A comparison of the two models will provide information that will identify the number of galaxies in our sample with true point sources. Our work expands on that of Ortiz et al. 2024, increasing the number of galaxies analyzed. We hope to determine if this method of identifying dust-obscured AGN is more comprehensive and effective than other methods and allows us to broaden the search for unresolved IR-luminous AGN.
